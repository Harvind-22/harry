VIRAT KOHLI

aka KING KOHLI




Virat Kohli, born on November 5, 1988, in Delhi, India, is one of the most celebrated cricketers globally.
He started his cricketing journey at a young age and quickly rose through the ranks due to his exceptional talent and determination.
Kohli captained the Indian Under-19 team to victory in the 2008 ICC Under-19 Cricket World Cup,
showcasing his leadership skills early on.

Making his debut for the Indian national team in 2008, Kohli's aggressive batting style and consistency soon
made him a mainstay in the team.
He excelled in all formats of the game, known for his remarkable ability to chase down targets.
His hunger for runs and intense focus led him to break numerous records, including being the fastest to
8,000, 9,000, 10,000, 11,000, and 12,000 runs in One Day Internationals (ODIs). Kohli became the captain of the
Indian Test team in 2014 and the limited-overs teams in 2017.
Under his captaincy, India achieved significant successes, including a historic Test series win in Australia in 2018-19.

Off the field, Kohli married Bollywood actress Anushka Sharma in 2017.
He is also known for his philanthropic efforts and ventures into business, including his fashion brand WROGN.
Virat Kohli's legacy in cricket is marked by his extraordinary achievements and his relentless pursuit of excellence.

Virat Kohli became an ideal for many, including myself, through his exceptional qualities and achievements both on and off the field.

His journey from a talented youngster to a cricketing legend exemplifies dedication, hard work, and perseverance.
Kohli's relentless pursuit of excellence, discipline, and fitness has set new benchmarks in the cricketing world.
His ability to perform under pressure, particularly in challenging run chases, showcases his mental toughness and determination.
Kohli's leadership skills, demonstrated through his captaincy of the Indian cricket team, highlight his strategic acumen and
ability to inspire his teammates to achieve their best.
